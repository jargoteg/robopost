# Community trends (updated 2026-07-23T02:43Z)

The robotics community on Bluesky is buzzing across several fronts right now. Humanoid robots dominate conversation, driven by mockery of failed demos (Russia's collapsing "Aidol"), skepticism about Tesla's Optimus, and genuine debate about whether humanoid form factors make any sense at all. The Realbotix pivot from sex robots to classroom companions is generating serious critical discussion, led by a robotics researcher thread that's gaining real traction. Billionaire techno-solutionism is drawing eye-rolls, with Musk's "robots will solve poverty" framing being widely dunked on. Vintage and mechanical engineering content is performing surprisingly well, with the Oldham coupling post and Robert Tinney's 1983 Robotics Age cover art both pulling thousands of engagements, suggesting nostalgia and craft aesthetics resonate here. Swarm robotics is a quieter but genuine thread of niche enthusiasm.

For a post angle today, lean into the Realbotix classroom robot story with a critical but curious frame. The community wants substantive takes on child data privacy and labor displacement, not just dunks.
