# Community trends (updated 2026-07-22T08:29Z)

The robotics community is buzzing around several overlapping conversations right now. The Realbotix pivot from sex robots to classroom AI assistants is generating serious discussion, particularly around the "Sally" humanoid being deployed in a New York school district this fall, with researchers and critics raising data privacy, labor replacement, and child safety concerns. Humanoid robots broadly remain a target of skepticism, with the viral Russian "Aidol" collapse and the recurring joke about expensive robots just being remote-controlled by humans fueling cynicism about industry hype. There's genuine engineering appreciation circulating for classical mechanisms like the Oldham coupling and folded structural designs in robotics contexts. Defense robotics got a quiet mention with Ukrainian UGV startup Trinity Robotics raising European funding. Swarm robotics is a smaller but active thread with PhD researchers trying to build community there.

A post angle that would land today: a sharp, good-faith breakdown of the Realbotix classroom deployment, framing the legitimate pedagogical questions separately from the obvious dunks. That nuance is currently missing from the conversation.
