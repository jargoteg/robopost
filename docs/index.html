One policy, three motion reference types, one real G1. M3imic argues the bottleneck was always the representational mismatch between joint angles and end-effector poses. Modality-specific encoders fix it. http://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/11576560

98.42% sim success on unseen motions is the headline. The question I'd push the authors on: how often does a human pose reference exceed the G1's joint limits in real trials, and what does the policy actually do when it does? That failure mode matters a lot for practical teleoperation.
Code is up at github.com/Renforce-Dynamics/MultiModalWBC and the arXiv preprint is open at arxiv.org/abs/2606.04829. If you're working on unified humanoid controllers, the encoder architecture here is worth reading closely before the IEEE paywall fully locks it.
