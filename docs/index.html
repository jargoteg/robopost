ROV resurveys cost thousands per hour. IBPA reconstructs a manifold mesh from streaming point clouds live and flags coverage gaps before you surface. Overhangs, verticals, complex topology included. https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.11627v1

The core move: ditching DTMs entirely. Height fields can't represent overhangs or vertical structures, which are everywhere in subsea inspection. BPA adapted to incremental streaming gives you actual topology. Hole detection tells the operator or planner where to steer next.

Why can't robots reorient a pen mid-grasp while humans do it without thinking? TeleDexter learns contact execution, not trajectory copying. 75%, baselines at zero, zero-shot real transfer. https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.11481v1

The clever bit: instead of imitating joint angles frame-by-frame, it tracks hand-object co-motion as subgoals. A hybrid sparse+dense reward handles diverse contact transitions in one RL stage. That's what gets you generalization across seven different tasks.
Honest caveat: zero-shot sim-to-real in a clean lab is not the same as an unstructured field environment. But the behavioral cloning downstream result is the sleeper finding here. Dexterous demo data scarcity is the real bottleneck. Does this finally move that needle?

Bipedal robots fail on loose ground because the terrain itself changes when you step on it. Georgia Tech's fix: cleat geometry that keeps substrate stress below yield threshold. 30-deg granular slopes, scales to 15 kg. Foot design before controller design. https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.11855v1

The cleat spacing result is the sharpest finding: too sparse and the sand liquefies, too dense and resistance spikes. Intermediate spacing is not a tuning trick, it is load distribution keeping the ground in a solid regime. That is terradynamics applied to robot design, not control.
Caveat worth naming: this is controlled lab sand. Field granular surfaces, scree, mine tailings, wet rubble, have moisture gradients and mixed grain sizes that shift yield thresholds unpredictably. Does the active cleat depth mechanism adapt fast enough? That is the paper I want to see next.
