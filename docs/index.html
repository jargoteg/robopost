Safe robots hurt fewer humans. The math to prove it is new. ergoCub co-optimizes hardware + motion control using spinal load on a nearby human as a design variable. One cost function, safer collaboration AND better walking. https://www.nature.com/articles/s42256-026-01272-2

The interesting claim is no trade-off: safety and locomotion robustness improve together. That's only possible if you optimize them jointly from the start, not patch one onto the other. Conceptually clean. The field test is the hard part.
No deployment numbers in the abstract. Humanoid safety papers from controlled labs have a rough track record in unstructured settings. How does a spinal-load cost function behave when the human moves unpredictably? That's the question I want answered. Open access preprint: arxiv.org/abs/2605.26991
