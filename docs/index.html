Nine modular mini-robots. No leader. Gait encoded as graph topology, signed edges tune phase from gallop to trot, and fault tolerance scales directly with graph degree. Worst-case phase error drops 3x vs leader-follower. https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.07281v1

The clever bit: you do not reprogram the gait, you rewire two signed inter-subgraph edges. That is a compact control layer. Physical demos are nine modules on flat floors though, so terrain scaling is still an open question.
The fault tolerance framing maps well onto real deployment anxiety: what happens when modules fail mid-task? Here graph degree gives you a direct handle on robustness budget. Worth asking: does this hold when module failures are correlated, not random?
