A gripper that wraps like a plant tendril just lifted a watermelon intact. No finger joints. Force distributes along the whole contact surface, not at two points. Clever bio-inspired mechanism. Field… https://robohub.org/vine-inspired-robotic-gripper-gently-lifts-heavy-and-fragile-objects/

The self-conforming wrap is the key idea here. Rigid grippers fail on soft produce because point forces bruise. Distributing load along continuous contact solves that without per-object tuning. Genuinely useful for irregular shapes.
What I want to see next: cycle count data, performance on wet or muddy surfaces, and grip reliability when the object orientation varies. Lab demos on clean watermelons are step one. Would this survive a commercial harvest line? That's the paper I'm waiting for.
