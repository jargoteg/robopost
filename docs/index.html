What if you never needed a real robot to collect robot data? RynnWorld-Teleop replaces the physical hardware with a generative world model. Hand poses in, egocentric video out, embodiment-agnostic labels for any robot. 40+ FPS on one H100. https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.06558

The clever part: the action label (hand pose stream) stays hardware-agnostic by design. One teleoperation session can retarget to multiple embodiments after the fact. That is the actual scaling argument, not the video quality.
The part I want to stress-test: 'zero-shot Sim2Real on dexterous bimanual tasks' with no task-level success numbers in the abstract. World models that look right but hallucinate contact physics are a known failure mode. Anyone seen the full results? What tasks did they actually test?

VLA models assume Markov. Every step, they forget. LaMem-VLA fixes this with dual latent memory tokens woven directly into the reasoning sequence, not appended as extra frames. Genuine architectural difference. https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.07608v1

The clever bit: retrieval feeds a condenser that outputs compact latent tokens, then a weaver injects them into the same embedding sequence as the current obs. Memory participates in reasoning, not just observation. That is not what most memory-augmented VLAs do.
Honest caveat: all evals are on SimplerEnv and LIBERO, both simulated. Real hardware with noisy high-freq sensors is a very different stress test. Does the dual-vault retrieval stay coherent there? That is the paper I want to see next.
