A 4-DoF robot arm just hit 20 consecutive catches in a shared 3-ball juggling cascade with a human. Previous record: 4. All 8 participants beat it within 10 min. Real hardware, real balls. https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15129v1

The system chains Kalman ball tracking + online multiple-shooting trajectory optimization + a state machine for timing. 75.1% on 3-ball cascades, 87.2% on single-ball. One person hit 100% over 40 throws. The numbers are solid.
The caveat the authors actually admit: no wrist orientation control on a 4-DoF arm, so throws into the rear workspace fail systematically. That's the kind of honest limitation section you rarely see. What's the real application you'd want this for, handoffs in assembly or surgical assist?
